I am a recognized expert with over 10 years of pioneering work in Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Tomography. My research has substantially contributed to advancing SAR applications in global forest biomass estimation and environmental monitoring. As one of the first scientists to integrate Deep Learning with SAR data for vegetation mapping, I have developed innovative methodologies that have set new standards in Earth observation. My career has been marked by transformative contributions, particularly to the European Space Agency’s BIOMASS mission, where my work on radar-based biomass estimation has facilitated crucial advances in forest biomass stock assessments.

Reseach achievements

Innovations in SAR and Deep Learning Integration 

  • Pioneering SAR-Deep Learning applications:
    Among the first researchers to apply Deep Learning to SAR for vegetation mapping, introducing enhanced radar applications for environmental monitoring and positioning SAR as a reliable tool for dense vegetation analysis.
  • Advanced SAR Tomography for biomass estimation:
    Developed state-of-the-art TomoSAR algorithms achieving biomass estimation errors as low as 15% in high-biomass regions, a critical factor in the selection of BIOMASS as ESA’s 7th Earth Explorer mission.
  • Tomographic phase linking improvements:
    Innovated the application of deep learning in tomographic phase linking, enhancing SAR performance in complex environments and improving signal reliability for large-scale deployments.

Interdisciplinary Contributions 

  • Integration of radar and optical data:
    Co-developed the TWINNS architecture, a dual neural network combining Sentinel-1 radar and Sentinel-2 optical time series for advanced land mapping. This breakthrough enhanced data fusion techniques for land cover analysis, bridging radar, optical remote sensing, and machine learning.
  • Biomass and canopy structure measurement:
    Contributed to the BIOMASS mission’s algorithm development, providing unique insights into high-biomass regions under defense radar restrictions, particularly crucial for tropical forest studies.

Selected Publications

I have established a highly independent research path. My groundbreaking work in Deep Learning applied to SAR, distinct from my PhD focus, is reflected in the increasing citations of my publications in this field, demonstrating my growing influence and leadership.

Deep Learning in SAR

  • D. Ho Tong Minh et al., "Deep Recurrent Neural Networks for Winter Vegetation Quality Mapping via Multitemporal SAR Sentinel-1", IEEE Letters Geoscience and Remote Sensing, 2018. (128 citations)

    I was the first SAR scientist to bring Deep Learning for vegetation mapping, highlighting the superior capabilities of radar data for winter vegetation monitoring in cloud-covered periods.

  • D. Ienco et al., Combining Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2 Satellite Image Time Series for land cover mapping via a multi-source deep learning architecture, ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing, 2019. (294 citations)

    I co-developed a deep learning architecture to combine Sentinel-1 radar and Sentinel-2 optical time series for land mapping.

  • E. Ndikumana et al., "Deep Recurrent Neural Network for Agricultural Classification using multitemporal SAR Sentinel-1 for Camargue, France ", Remote Sensing, 2018. (285 citations)

    I co-demonstrated Deep Recurrent Neural Networks to enhance radar-based crop mapping, positioning them as crucial tools for future remote sensing time series analysis.

  • D. Ho Tong Minh et al., "Interferometric Phase Linking: Algorithm, application, and perspective," in IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Magazine, 2023. (10 citations)

    My efforts explore using Deep Learning to enhance tomographic phase linking techniques, improving performance in complex signal environments.

Forest Biomass Monitoring

  • S. Quegan et al., The European Space Agency BIOMASS mission: measuring forest above–ground biomass from space. Remote Sensing of Environment, 2019. (258 citations)

    We introduced the P-band BIOMASS mission, which aims to map global above-ground biomass and reduce uncertainties in carbon stock and flux calculations.

  • D. Ho Tong Minh et al., SAR tomography for the retrieval of forest biomass and height: Cross-validation at two tropical forest sites in French Guiana, Remote Sensing of Environment, 2016. (cited by 166)

    I developed SAR Tomography methods to estimate tropical forest biomass with errors as low as 15% in high-biomass and estimated top-of-canopy height within a 2.5m deviation.

  • J.M.B. Carreiras et al., Coverage of high biomass forests by the ESA BIOMASS mission under defense restrictions. Remote Sensing of Environment, 2017. (112 citations)

    We showed the tropical forest biome will experience minimal data loss under Space Objects Tracking Radar restrictions.

SAR tomography and interferometry

  • D. Ho Tong Minh et al., Relating P-band synthetic aperture radar tomography to tropical forest biomass. Remote Sensing. 2013. (205 citations)

    I found that the radar signal from about 30 meters above the ground has the best correlation and sensitivity to forest biomass from 250 to 450 Mg/ha as per a forest growth model.

  • D. Ho Tong Minh et al., " Radar interferometry: 20 years of development in time series techniques and future perspectives. Remote Sensing, 2020. (97 citations)

    I explored challenges related to Big Data in processing SAR Inteferometry for precise long-term monitoring of ground deformation and environmental changes.

  • Y-N Ngo et al., "Tropical forest vertical structure characterization: From GEDI to P-band SAR tomography," in IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Letters, 2022. (21 citations)

    We highlight the potential of GEDI LiDAR to calibrate future BIOMASS mission algorithms for forest structure assessment.
